How The Force Sensor Works?

Load Cell: A load cell converts the mechanical force of a strike into an electrical signal. It usually consists of strain gauges, which deform slightly under pressure. This deformation causes a change in electrical resistance, which the sensor converts into a measurable voltage. The Arduino microcontroller then reads this voltage and translates it into a force value, usually expressed in newtons (N).



Force-Sensitive Resistor (FSR): An FSR operates by changing its resistance when pressure is applied. The more force exerted on the sensor, the lower the resistance becomes, which the Arduino microcontroller can measure as a change in voltage. This data is then used to calculate the force of the impact.
